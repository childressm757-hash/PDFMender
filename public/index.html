COMMON CAUSES

Why CM/ECF and State eFile Systems Reject PDFs

  • Interactive or AcroForm form fields embedded in the document
  • XFA-based dynamic form architecture
  • Encryption or document security restrictions
  • Embedded JavaScript or active content
  • Attachments embedded inside the PDF

Federal and state filing systems typically require static, non-interactive PDFs to ensure reliable processing and archival integrity.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Why are interactive PDFs rejected by CM/ECF?

Many federal courts using CM/ECF require static PDF documents. Interactive or XFA-based PDFs may fail automated processing.

What does “PDF must be flattened” mean?

Flattening converts interactive form fields into static page content. Courts often require flattened documents prior to filing.

Why are encrypted PDFs rejected?

Filing systems may block password-protected or restricted PDFs because they interfere with scanning and archival processes.

Does this work for state courts?

The tool addresses common technical rejection causes across major state eFile systems such as Texas, New York (NYSCEF), California, Delaware, and Florida.
