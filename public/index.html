COMMON CAUSES

Why CM/ECF and State eFile Systems Reject PDFs

  • Interactive or AcroForm form fields embedded in the document
  • XFA-based dynamic form architecture
  • Encryption or document security restrictions
  • Embedded JavaScript or active content
  • Attachments embedded inside the PDF

Filing systems often require static, non-interactive PDFs to support scanning and archival integrity.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Why are interactive PDFs rejected by CM/ECF?

Many federal courts require static PDFs. Interactive or XFA-based PDFs may fail automated processing.

What does “PDF must be flattened” mean?

Flattening converts interactive fields into static page content. Courts often require flattening before filing.

Why are encrypted PDFs rejected?

Filing systems may block password-protected or restricted PDFs because they interfere with scanning and archival workflows.

Does this work for state courts?

PDFMender addresses common technical rejection causes across major state eFile systems (Texas, New York, California, Delaware, Florida). Rules vary by jurisdiction.
